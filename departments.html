Departments

The Ministry operates through several departments, each focused on a specific aspect of internal affairs, security and community welfare.

Our Structure

Ministry Departments

Each department is led by a Director or Commissioner and is responsible for implementing the Ministry's mandate in its specific domain.

Amnesty Commission

The Amnesty Commission implements Uganda's Amnesty Act, offering amnesty to Ugandans who have engaged in acts of rebellion. It promotes peace and reconciliation by facilitating the demobilisation, reintegration and resettlement of former combatants.

Learn More →

Department of Community Service

The Department of Community Service administers non-custodial sentences, giving offenders the opportunity to give back to society through supervised community work. It reduces prison overcrowding while promoting rehabilitation.

Visit Website →

Finance & Administration

The Finance and Administration Department ensures the proper management of the Ministry's financial resources, human capital, and administrative functions. It ensures accountability, transparency and efficiency across all Ministry units.

Learn More →

Government Security Office

The Government Security Office (GSO) is responsible for the regulation and control of explosives, weapons and related materials. It issues blasting permits, supervises security companies and advises the Ministry on matters of explosives safety.

Learn More →

National Focal Point

The National Focal Point on Small Arms and Light Weapons (NFP) coordinates Uganda's efforts to control the proliferation of small arms and light weapons (SALW). It implements international conventions and promotes arms management best practices.

Learn More →

Planning and Policy Analysis

The Planning and Policy Analysis Department provides strategic direction to the Ministry by developing evidence-based policies, plans and programmes. It monitors implementation, evaluates outcomes and provides advisory services to Ministry leadership.

Learn More →

Counter-Trafficking in Persons

The Counter-Trafficking in Persons (COTIP) Unit coordinates Uganda's multi-sectoral response to human trafficking. It prosecutes traffickers, protects and supports victims, and leads prevention campaigns across the country.
